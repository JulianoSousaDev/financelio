# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Young professionals (25-35) managing personal finances for the first time seriously. They open Financelio on their phone during a commute, at a coffee shop, or before bed. They want to understand where their money goes without feeling overwhelmed by spreadsheets or budgeting jargon. Their context is quick glances and occasional deep dives, not marathon data-entry sessions.

## Product Purpose

Financelio is a personal finance companion that turns raw transaction data into clarity. It exists because most people know they should track spending but abandon complex tools after a week. Success means the user opens the app, sees their financial picture in under 5 seconds, and makes one informed decision they wouldn't have otherwise made. The app supports individual tracking and optional family mode for shared finances.

## Brand Personality

**Minimal. Precise. Professional.**

Voice: direct, never condescending. Tone is confident but not cold. Numbers speak louder than words. Every pixel earns its place. The interface should feel like opening a well-organized notebook, not a control room.

Emotional goals: trust, clarity, control. The user should feel competent, not lectured.

## Anti-references

- **Spreadsheet-style complexity**: No overwhelming grids, dense tables, or 15-field forms. Financelio is not Excel with a mobile skin.
- **Monzo-style gamification**: No confetti, streaks, achievements, or cheerful animations for basic tasks. Tracking money is not a game.
- **Crypto-trader dark mode**: No neon accents, terminal aesthetics, or "number go up" energy. This is personal finance, not speculation.
- **SaaS dashboard clichés**: No hero-metric cards with gradient accents, identical icon grids, or modal-first interactions.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over decoration.** Every element serves understanding. If a visual doesn't help the user read their financial state faster, remove it.
2. **Progressive disclosure.** Show the essential (balance, trend) immediately. Details appear on demand, not by default.
3. **Respect the number.** Financial data has gravity. Treat monetary values with typographic precision (tabular-nums, semantic colors, right-aligned). Never truncate or approximate.
4. **Friction where it matters.** Destructive actions (delete transaction, leave family) require confirmation. Everything else should be one tap away.
5. **Consistent rhythm.** Spacing, typography, and color follow the design system without exception. Consistency builds trust; inconsistency signals carelessness.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Minimum touch target 44px. Semantic color usage (green for income, red for expense, never reversed). Light and dark mode both fully functional. Text contrast ratios validated. No information conveyed by color alone (icons and labels supplement).
